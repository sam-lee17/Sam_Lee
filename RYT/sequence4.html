Salamba Sirsasana (Supported Headstand Pose)

Anatomic and energetic benefits of the pose:

Salamba Sirsasana strengthens the arms, core, legs, and spine, stimulates the pituitary and pineal glands, improves digestion, and alleviates symptoms of menopause. It also calms the brain and helps relieve stress and mild depression. The pose can be therapeutically applied to asthma, infertility, insomnia, and sinusitis.

Safety concerns with this pose:

Loss of balance can lead to a back injury, which could be prevented by practicing against the wall. If you don’t firmly press the ground with your elbows or use strength in the core, it may give too much burden to one’s neck. I can instruct students to tuck their chin in and use strength in their arms and core for support. 

Common alignment issues (tightness in muscles, muscle weakness, instability, skeletal issues):

The pose may be extremely unstable if your arm, leg, core, and spine muscles are weak. It is important to align the body so that it won’t put too much pressure on one specific muscle.

Salamba Sirsasana requires a great amount of strength in the arms, legs, core, and spine. We can strengthen the arms and legs by Adho Mukha Svanasana. We can enhance the core and back muscles by Phalakasana, Vasisthasana, and Navasana. Although Tittibhasana is an advanced posture, if possible, this could strengthen all of the crucial muscles needed for Salamba Sirsasana.

 

Theme: Balance / Self-Control

Warm-up: 10 min

Savasana - Supta Virasana (right; with left leg straight) - put left foot on the right thigh and pull the left knee toward the ground - Supta Virasana (left; with right leg straight) - put left foot on the left thigh and pull the right knee toward the ground - Savasana - Supta Padangusthasana (right; twist to the left and right) - Supta Padangusthasana (left; twist to the left and right) - Panwanmuktasana

Working towards the peak pose: 25 min

Matsyasana (with feet on the floor)  - straighten the legs - Uttana Padasana - Setu Bandha Sarvangasana (with shoulders tucked under the back) - Dvicakravahanasana (do 15; repeat twice) - Vajrasana - Adho Mukha Svanasana - Virabhadrasana I (right) - Virabhadrasana III (right) - Virabhadrasana II (right) - Viparita Virabhadrasana (right) - Adho Mukha Svanasana - Virabhadrasana I (left) - Virabhadrasana III (left) - Virabhadrasana II (left) - Viparita Virabhadrasana (left) - Adho Mukha Svanasana

Peak Pose: 5 min

Adho Mukha Vajrasana - Salamba Sirsasana - Adho Mukha Vajrasana (short relaxation) - Salamba Sirsasana 

Cooldown: 10 min

Adho Mukha Vajrasana - Balasana - Baddha Konasana - Agnistambhasana (right) - Agnistambhasana (left) - Ardha Virasana (right) - Ardha Virasana (left) - Virasana - Paschimottanasana


Savasana: 10 min

Hanumanasana (Divine Monkey Pose)

Anatomic and energetic benefits:

Hanumanasana strengthens and stretches both front (quadriceps) and back (hamstrings and groins) of the thighs. It stimulates the abdominal organs. Also, the pose allows students to ground themselves and balance the nervous system.

Safety concerns with this pose:

If you overdo the pose beyond your capabilities, you might injure hamstrings or groins. Students can start from Ardha Hanumanasana or place a bolster/block under the pelvis if they have tight leg muscles. Since the control of the breathing is essential in this pose, I could instruct students to lengthen in inhale and deepen the stretch in exhale. 

Common alignment issues (tightness in muscles, muscle weakness, instability, skeletal issues):

Students might experience tightness in hamstring or groin muscles, muscle weakness in quadriceps, and instability resulting from alignment issues in the pelvis.

Hanumanasana requires strength in the front leg, especially the quadriceps, and flexibility in the back leg, including hamstrings and groins. Paschimottanasana, Supta Padangusthasana, and Uttanasana would stretch the back of the legs. Parsvottanasana and Prasarita Padottanasana would help strengthen the hamstrings and stretch the back of the legs simultaneously.

 

Theme: Honoring the Journey

Warm-up: 10 min

Dandasana - Paschimottanasana - Janu Sirsasana (right) - Baddha Konasana - Janu Sirsasana (left) - Paschimottanasana - Janu Sirsasana (right) - Baddha Konasana - Janu Sirsasana (left) - Paschimottanasana

Working towards the peak pose: 25 min

Sun Salutations A (Tadasana - Urdhva Hastasana - Uttanasana - Ardha Uttanasana; repeat 10 times) - Adho Mukha Svanasana - Eka Pada Rajkapotasana (right) - Lakini Pose (twist to the left) - Vanmadevasana (just the back leg) - Sleeping Pigeon Pose (right) - Adho Mukha Svanasana - Eka Pada Rajkapotasana (left) - Lakini Pose (twist to the right) - Vanmadevasana (just the back leg) - Sleeping Pigeon Pose (left) - Adho Mukha Svanasana - Adho Mukha Vajrasana

Peak pose: 5 min

Ardha Hanumanasna (right) - Hanumanasna (right) - Ardha Hanumanasna (left) - Hanumanasna (left) - Adho Mukha Svanasana - Adho Mukha Vajrasana

Cooldown: 10 min

Balasana - Virasana - Supta Virasana - Supta Baddha Konasana - Supta Padangusthasana A + B (right) - Supta Padangusthasana A + B (left) - Supta Padangusthasana A + B (right) - Jathara Parivartanasana (right) - Supta Padangusthasana A + B (left) - Jathara Parivartanasana (left) - Pawanmuktasana (right; with left leg straight) - Pawanmuktasana (left; with right leg straight) - Pawanmuktasana - Navasana - Paschimottanasana


Savasana: 10 min

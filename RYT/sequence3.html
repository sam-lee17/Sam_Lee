Eka Pada Raja Kapotasana (Mermaid Pose)

Anatomic and energetic benefits:

Eka Pada Raja Kapotasana opens the hip joints and the chest and stretches the quadriceps, groins, spine, and shoulders. The pose helps to relieve back pain and sciatica and stimulates internal organs of the body. Energetic benefits include reducing negative feelings like fear, anxiety, and stress, and stimulating positive energy in the body.

Safety concerns with this pose:

They might injure their back or shoulders if they have tight muscles in these areas. It is significant to go through blueprint postures before reaching this peak pose.

Common alignment issues (tightness in muscles, muscle weakness, instability, skeletal issues):

Students may not be able to anchor their foot on their arms, which would happen as a result of tightness in the shoulders and ankles. In this case, they can use a strap to lengthen their quadriceps. Balancing might be an issue if their hips are tight or not aligned correctly.

Eka Pada Raja Kapotasana requires strength in the core and pelvic floor for stability, and flexibility in the shoulders, chest, quadriceps, hip flexors, and ankles. We can stretch the chest, shoulders, and abdomen through Urdha Mukha Svanasana, Bhujangasana, and Raja Kapotasana. Hip openers such as Baddha Konasana, Kurmasana, Malasana could open the hips flexors. We can lengthen the ankles by Virasana

 

Theme: Harmony

Warm-up: 10 min

Baddha Konasana - Ardha Padmasana (right; fold forward) - Bharadvajasana (right; keeping the Ardha Padmasana legs) - Ardha Padmasana (left; fold forward) - Bharadvajasana (left; keeping the Ardha Padmasana legs) - Bharadvajasana (right; fold forward) - Baddha Konasana - Bharadvajasana (left; fold forward) - Baddha Konasana

Working towards the peak pose: 20 min

Vajrasana - Virasana - Supta Virasana - push the pelvic bone forward - Ustrasana - Balasana - Adho Mukhasana - Phalakasana - Ardha Bhujangasana (place elbows on the floor) - Ardha Bhekasana (right) - Ardha Bhekasana (left) - Bhujangasana (slowly move the gaze toward the ceiling) - Rajakapotasana - Ardha Danurasana (right) - Ardha Danurasana (left) - Bhujangasana (gaze toward the ceiling) - Makarasana (short relaxation) 

Peak Pose: 10 min

Adho Mukha Svanasana - Eka Pada Raja Kapotasana (right; with the back leg on the floor, arch back) - Lakini Pose (twist to the left) - Vanmadevasana (just the back leg) - Eka Pada Raja Kapotasana (right) - Adho Mukha Svanasana - Eka Pada Raja Kapotasana (left; with the back leg on the floor, arch back) - Lakini Pose (twist to the right) - Vanmadevasana (just the back leg) - Eka Pada Raja Kapotasana (left) - Adho Mukha Svanasana

Cooldown: 10 min

Vajrasana - Paschimottanasana - Marichyasana (right) - Marichyasana (left) - Marichyasana (right) - keep the straight leg and put the other leg onto top of it so that the knees would be on top of each other (left; one leg in Paschimottanasana and the other in Gomukhasana) - Gomukhasana (right) - Marichyasana (left) - one leg in Paschimottanasana and the other in Gomukhasana (left) - Gomukhasana (left) - Baddha Konasana

Savasana: 10 min

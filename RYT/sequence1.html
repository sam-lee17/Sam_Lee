Urdhva Dhanurasana (Wheel Pose)

Anatomic and energetic benefits of the pose:

The pose opens up the chest, strengthens the arms, wrists, legs, buttocks, quadriceps, abdomen, and spine. It also increases energy and counteracts depression.

Safety concerns with this pose:

Tight muscles in the back may lead to spine injury. This pose should be done after lengthening the spine first through different backbend postures. People with carpal tunnel syndrome or wrist injuries should avoid this pose or support the hands on the blocks. Students should make sure they tuck their chins when coming back from the posture to prevent any neck or head injuries.

Common alignment issues (tightness in muscles, muscle weakness, instability, skeletal issues):

Common alignment issues of Urdhva Dhanurasana may include tightness in wrists, shoulders, spine muscles. Muscle weakness in the quadriceps and arms and a lack of openness in the chest could create instability.

The pose requires strength in the spine, legs, and arms. It also requires flexibility in the spine, shoulders, and wrists. Backbends such as Bhujangasana, Ustrasana, Kapotasana, Dhanurasana would help strengthen the back and the opening in the chest. Dhanurasana and Gomukhasana would allow lateral rotation of the shoulder muscles. We could develop the strength in the legs by Setu Bhanda Sarvangasana, Ustrasana, and Anjaneyasana.

 

Theme: Opening up

Warm-up: 10 min

Baddha Konasana - Upavista Konasana (90 degrees) - Parivrtta Janu Sirsasana (right; with elbows on the floor) - Parivrtta Janu Sirsasana (left; with elbows on the floor) - Upavista Konasana (90 degrees) - Parivrtta Janu Sirsasana (right) - Parivrtta Janu Sirsasana (left) - Baddha Konasana

Working towards the peak pose: 20 min

Vajrasana - Parvatasana - Bitalasana + Marjariasana (repeat 5 times) - Adho Mukha Vajrasana - Adho Mukha Svanasana - Bhujangasana - Tiryaka Bhujangasana - Dhanurasana - Bhujangasana (gaze toward the ceiling) - Ardha Rajakapotasana (bend the knees half way) - Adho Mukha Vajrasana - Vajrasana - Gomukhasana - Ustrasana

Peak pose: 5 min

Setu Bhanda Sarvangasana - Urdva Dhanurasana - Savasana (short relaxation) - Setu Bhanda Sarvangasana - Urdva Dhanurasana - Savasana (short relaxation)

Cooldown: 15 min

Paschimottanasana - Upavista Konasana (120 degrees) - Parivrtta Janu Sirsasana (right) - Yogi Dandasana (right) - Parivrtta Janu Sirsasana (left) - Yogi Dandasana (left) - Baddha Konasana - Gomukhasana (right; bend forward) - Gomukhasana (left; bend forward) - Ardha Atsyendrasana(right) -  Ardha Atsyendrasana(left) - Halasana


Savasana: 10 min

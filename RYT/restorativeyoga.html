Chair & Restorative Yoga

August 6, 2021
Other(Teaching)

Chair Yoga 

Things to consider regarding chair yoga poses:

What is the point of the pose?
What are the lines of energy?
Where is the stability?
Where is the flexibility?
Where is the softening?
Is it safe?

Things to consider regarding chair yoga clients:

What are the injuries?
What are the core strength issues?
Are there any over-tight muscles?
Are there any unstable joints?
Does this client like to be touched?

Chair Yoga Sequence Example

Pranayama - Sitting Surya Namaskar - Twists - Leg stretches - Hamstring stretches - Outer hip stretches - Core strengtheners - Purvottanasana - Ustrasana - Warrior series and Parsvakonasana - Balasana - Ending Meditation - Savasana in Balasana

 

Restorative Yoga

- intended to activate the body’s full relaxation response and deepest opening
- student’s body is supported in every way possible to reach the total relaxation
- props include bolsters, blocks, blankets, straps, sandbags, eye pillows, wall, or chair
- full relaxation state enables the body to drop down into the parasympathetic nervous system, decreasing stress in the body, mind, and spirit and triggers the body’s healing force
- directed for everyone, but especially great for people who struggle with physical and mental health
- provide assistance to students in establishing the proper base of support than providing adjustments
- leave some room for silence during the practice
- can use essential oil

Benefits: 

- decreases the stress hormone cortisol
- improves sleep, digestion, and immunity
- releases tense muscles and aching joints
- teaches conscious control of relaxation
- increases concentration and focus
- activates the relaxation response<

Restorative Yoga Sequence Example #1

Heart Opening Sequence: supported heart opener - supported bridge - supported shoulder stand - supported reclining hero’s pose (peak pose) - supported forward fold (counterpose) - supported savasana

Restorative Yoga Sequence #2

Sequence to uplift: prostration - supported fish pose - legs up the wall (peak pose) - wide legs up the wall - reclining with knee support (counterpose) - supported savasana

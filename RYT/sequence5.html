Parivrtta Parsvakonasana (Revolved Extended Side Angle Posture)

Anatomic and energetic benefits:

Anatomic advantages include strengthening the legs, knees, abdomen, and ankles, stretching in groins, spine, chest, shoulders, improvement in digestion and balance. The pose also increases stamina. It is therapeutic for constipation, infertility, low backache, osteoporosis, sciatica, and menstrual discomfort.

Safety concerns with this pose:

Students with a neck injury should be cautious with Parivrtta Parsvakonasana. I could instruct them to avoid looking upward to see the top of the arm. Instead, they could look straight ahead and lengthen the neck evenly or look down.

Common alignment issues (tightness in muscles, muscle weakness, instability, skeletal issues):

Muscle weakness in leg muscles could make this posture unstable. Tight abdominal muscles could prevent students from twisting their torso.

Parivrtta Parsvakonasana requires strength in the legs, ankles, knees, and core and flexibility in the groins, spine, chest, abdomen, and shoulders. We can lengthen these muscles by Marichyasana or Ardha Atsyendrasana. We can strengthen the legs by Paruvrrta Trikonasana Trikonasana, Virabhadrasana I, and Virabhadrasana II. Virasana can strengthen ankles and knees.

 

Theme: Focusing on the inner self

Warm-up: 10 min

Paschimottanasana (legs hip-width apart; twist to the right and left, keeping the legs straight; repeat 2 times) - Ardha Purvottanasana - put hips on the floor - tuck the right shoulder against the right knee, right backhand pressing onto the flank area, stretch the outside of the shoulder and the back - do the same on the left side - Paschimottanasana (legs hip-width apart; twist to the right and left, keeping the legs straight) - Ardha Purvottanasana

Working towards the peak pose: 25 min

Tadasana - Utkatasana - Vrksasana (right) - Vrksasana (left) - Utthita Hasta Padangustasana (right) - Parivrtta Hasta Pangusthasana (right) - Utthita Hasta Padangustasana (left) - Parivrtta Hasta Pangusthasana (left) - Prasarita Padottasana - Trikonasana (right) - Utthita Trikonasana (right) - Parivrtta Trikonasana (right) - come up - Virabhadrasana (right) - Utthita Trikonasana (right) - Trikonasana (left) - Utthita Trikonasana (left) - Parivrtta Trikonasana (left) - come up - Virabhadrasana (left) - Utthita Trikonasana (left) - Prasarita Padottasana

Peak pose: 5 min

Vajrasana - Adho Mukha Vajrasana (relax here) - Adho Mukha Svanasana - Ashta Chandrasana (right; arch back) - Parivrtta Parsvakonasana (right) - Adho Mukha Svanasana - Ashta Chandrasana (left) - Parivrtta Parsvakonasana (left) - Adho Mukha Svanasana

Cooldown: 10 min

Vajrasana - Bitalasana + Marjariasana (repeat 5 times) - Anahatasana - go on the elbows - Mandukasana - Vajrasana - Agnistambhasana (bend forward) - Baddha Konasana - Paschimottanasana

 

Savasana: 10 min
